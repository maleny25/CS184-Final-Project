CS 184 Final Project Proposal

Kiwi Vine Growth Simulation

Angela Gao, Maleny Ruiz, Lucy Wan, and Nicole Wang

Summary

For our CS 184 final project, we are planning to create a kiwi vine growth simulation. We plan to make it look realistic and use L-Systems to simulate the growth.

Problem Description

We are planning to tackle this problem of simulating accurate growth of a kiwi vine. After researching multiple ideas, we landed on this based on the research that has already been done. We want to learn and apply the mathematical models that are used to simulate the growth of the kiwi vine. This project will simulate the physics from nature of the growth process. It will be a challenge for us because we have to learn about L-Systems to be able to simulate the growth. We are planning to tackle this problem by breaking it into parts of encoding accurate plant structure (including branching and leaf growth), rendering the plant successfully, and incorporating environmental factors.

Goals and Deliverables

The main goal of our project is to create an accurate simulation of kiwi plant growth in average conditions. This can be broken down into several parts: encoding accurate plant structure (including branching and leaf growth), rendering the plant successfully, and incorporating environmental factors.

Much of our work will be based on the equations and model published in the paper, “A functional-structural kiwifruit vine model integrating architecture, carbon dynamics and effects of the environment”. Several images from this model are shown below. We hope to successfully recreate renderings like this, and if that is successful, we will implement a growing model that may be adjusted in real time with carbon inputs. If things go smoothly with these two main goals, we will then adapt our code to simulate other plants given certain governing growth equations. This would be possible through further research of plant architecture and growing patterns, but we may not have time to accomplish.

Our simulation's success will be mostly determined through qualitative judgements, although we can compare it to the paper's model under standard lighting conditions to ensure the two plants' growth occurs at the same rate.

If we finish our goal ahead of schedule, we have several possible extensions. We hope to implement at least one of the ideas. One is adding lighting conditions such as outdoor lighting vs indoor lighting, another one would be to simulate the outdoor light of different times of the day. A second would be adding on vegetative growth and fruit growth on our models. This means that fruits will appear at the right time steps and grow with the rest of the plant at a reasonable rate.

Schedule

Week 1: Create Github repository, get familiar with WebGL and L-systems (get basic tree growth to work), create T structure which will be the starting point of our vine
Week 2: Shoot Development and Vine Architecture - create nodes and generate new nodes using the markov chains stated in the paper referenced
Week 3: Carbon Dynamics and Root growth - Use the Euler method as stated in the paper referenced to simulate carbon flow which will determine the flux of root growth
Week 4: Vegetative Growth and Fruit Growth (if time permits)
